The Central Dogma of Molecular Biology

This simulation visualizes the core processes of molecular biology: DNA transcription and RNA translation. These processes form the central dogma of molecular biology, describing the flow of genetic information within a biological system.

Key components:

  • DNA: The genetic blueprint, composed of ATCG bases.
  • Transcription: DNA is transcribed into messenger RNA (mRNA) by RNA polymerase.
  • mRNA: Carries the genetic information from DNA to the ribosome.
  • Translation: mRNA is translated into a polypeptide chain by ribosomes.
  • tRNA: Transfer RNA brings amino acids to the ribosome during translation.
  • Codons: Three-base sequences in mRNA that code for specific amino acids.
